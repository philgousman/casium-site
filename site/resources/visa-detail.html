O-1 Visa

Individuals with Extraordinary Ability

The O-1 is for people who sit at the very top of their field — the scientists, founders, engineers, artists, and athletes whose record of achievement puts them in a small percentage of those who have risen to the top. It rewards a track record, not a job title.

Overview

The O-1 is a temporary work visa for individuals with extraordinary ability in the sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics — demonstrated by sustained national or international acclaim.

Unlike the H-1B, the O-1 has no annual cap and no lottery, so it can be filed at any time of year. It is also not tied to a specific degree; what matters is evidence of a genuine record of achievement. That makes it a common choice for startup founders, senior researchers, and specialized engineers who may not fit neatly into a cap-subject category.

An O-1 requires a U.S. petitioner — an employer or an agent — but the agent structure means O-1 holders can work for multiple employers or clients, which is why it suits founders and consultants well.

Casium tip. The O-1 is often a stepping stone to an EB-1A green card — the two share the same “extraordinary ability” standard, so the evidence you build for one supports the other. We plan both together from day one.

Who qualifies

There are two O-1 sub-categories. O-1A covers the sciences, education, business, and athletics. O-1B covers the arts and the motion-picture / television industry. Most Casium clients file under O-1A.

To qualify under O-1A, you must meet at least three of the following eight criteria (a one-time major award like a Nobel or Olympic medal satisfies the standard on its own):

01
Nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence.
02
Membership in associations that require outstanding achievement, judged by experts.
03
Published material about you in professional or major trade publications.
04
Serving as a judge of the work of others in your field.
05
Original scientific, scholarly, or business contributions of major significance.
06
Authorship of scholarly articles in your field.
07
Employment in a critical or essential capacity for distinguished organizations.
08
A high salary or other remuneration relative to others in the field.

Evidence you’ll need

A strong O-1 petition is built on documentary proof, not adjectives. Your case team assembles a filing that typically includes:

Core evidence

Award certificates, press coverage, citation records, patent filings, membership confirmations, and pay records that map directly to the criteria above.

Advisory opinion

A written opinion from a peer group, labor organization, or expert in your field confirming the nature of the work. This is a required part of the filing.

Letters of recommendation

Independent expert letters (usually 5–8) from people who can speak to the significance of your contributions — including some who have not worked with you directly.

The consultation & itinerary

A description of the work you’ll do in the U.S., its dates, and the terms of your engagement or employment.
